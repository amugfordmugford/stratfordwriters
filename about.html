About the Group

A little history, and everything you need to know before your first visit.

Our story

The Writers Circle began in 2018 as a handful of neighbours who wanted a regular excuse to share their work and get honest, encouraging feedback. What started as five people around a kitchen table has grown into a warm, consistent group of writers spanning genres, ages, and experience levels.

We believe that writing improves in community. A fresh pair of eyes catches what yours can't. A well-timed question unlocks a chapter that felt stuck for months. And knowing that others will read your pages next Tuesday is, frankly, the best motivation there is.

There are no gatekeepers here — no published credits required, no genres favoured over others. Fiction, memoir, poetry, screenwriting, creative non-fiction: all of it is welcome at the table.

How our meetings work

We meet twice a month. The first meeting of each month is our open critique session: members share pages they've written since we last met (typically 3–5 pages), and the group responds with structured, constructive feedback. The second meeting rotates between themed prompt sessions, craft discussions, and occasional guest speakers.

We ask that feedback always lead with what's working before turning to suggestions. Growth happens best in a safe room.
